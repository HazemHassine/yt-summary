Apple's AI Software: A Crutch for the Unwilling?

Introduction

In a candid discussion, Bridget Cary shares her experience with Apple's new AI software and expresses her disappointment with its portrayed benefits. She criticizes the ads promoting this feature, showcasing users who are untruthful, unempathetic, and struggling to accomplish basic tasks. These characters appear to be using AI as a crutch to compensate for their shortcomings, leading Bridget to question Apple's faith in their customers.

Critique of Apple's Ads

Bridget discusses several Apple advertisements that feature individuals benefiting from Apple Intelligence, a trait she finds annoying and unrepresentative. These characters lack effort and dedication, yet somehow manage to get away with pretending to be knowledgeable or thoughtful thanks to Apple's AI. She notices a pattern of faking accomplishments and lying in multiple television commercials and invites her audience to compare these moments for themselves on YouTube and websites showing multiple iPhone advertisements.

Negative Personal Experience with Apple Intelligence

After enabling Apple Intelligence summaries on her Apple-based device for several weeks, Bridget notes that the feature did not significantly improve her life. Instead, it often provided robotically phrased summaries that failed to accurately grasp the original message. Upon expanding the message, she felt like she was needlessly filtering unnecessary information. As a result, she encourages others not to use this feature.
