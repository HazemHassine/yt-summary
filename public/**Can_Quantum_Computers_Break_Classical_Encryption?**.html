**Introduction to Quantum Computing**

The video begins by introducing the concept of quantum computing and its basic principles. **Classical Encryption Methods**

The speaker discusses classical encryption methods, including symmetric-key and public-key cryptography. Symmetric-key cryptography uses the same key for both encryption and decryption, while public-key cryptography uses a pair of keys: a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption. The video highlights the importance of these methods in securing online transactions and communications.

**Quantum Computers and Classical Encryption**

The speaker explains that quantum computers can potentially break classical encryption methods using algorithms developed by Peter Shor and Lov Grover. Shor's algorithm can factor large numbers exponentially faster than classical computers, which could render RSA encryption (a widely used public-key encryption method) vulnerable. Grover's algorithm, on the other hand, can search an unsorted database in less time than a classical computer.

**Limitations of Quantum Computers**

However, the speaker also notes that quantum computers have limitations when it comes to breaking classical encryption. The main challenges are:

  • Scalability: Currently, quantum computers are not scalable to the level required to break most classical encryption methods.
  • Error Correction: Quantum computers are prone to errors, which can make it difficult to maintain the fragile quantum states required for computation.
  • Quantum Noise Reduction: The noisy nature of quantum computers can make it challenging to obtain accurate results.

**Post-Quantum Cryptography**

Given the potential risks, researchers are exploring new cryptographic methods that are resistant to quantum attacks, known as post-quantum cryptography. Some of these methods include:

  • Lattice-based cryptography: This method uses the hardness of problems related to lattices to build resistant cryptographic systems.
  • Code-based cryptography: This approach uses the hardness of decoding problems to build cryptographic systems.
  • Hash-based signatures: This method uses the properties of cryptographic hash functions to build resistant signature schemes.

The video concludes that quantum computers have the potential to break classical encryption methods, but there are limitations to their capabilities.
