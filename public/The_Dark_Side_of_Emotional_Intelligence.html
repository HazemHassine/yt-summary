Video Overview

In this video, the speaker explores the concept of emotional intelligence and its potential dark side. Emotional intelligence refers to the ability to recognize and understand emotions in oneself and others, and to use this awareness to guide thought and behavior.

The Original Intent of Emotional Intelligence

The concept of emotional intelligence was first introduced by Peter Salovey and John D. Mayer in 1990, and popularized by Daniel Goleman in his 1995 book "Emotional Intelligence: Why It Can Matter More Than IQ." The original intent of emotional intelligence was to describe a set of skills that could help individuals better navigate social situations, build stronger relationships, and achieve greater success in their personal and professional lives.

The Misuse of Emotional Intelligence

However, the speaker argues that emotional intelligence can also be misused. For example, highly manipulative individuals can use their emotional intelligence to exploit and manipulate others for their own gain. This can include using emotional intelligence to:

  • Manipulate others' emotions: By understanding how others feel, highly manipulative individuals can use this knowledge to manipulate others into doing what they want.
  • Build fake relationships: Emotional intelligence can be used to build superficial relationships that are designed to benefit the manipulative individual, rather than being genuinely invested in the other person.
  • Make others feel guilty or ashamed: Highly manipulative individuals can use emotional intelligence to make others feel guilty or ashamed, as a way of controlling or manipulating them.

The Dark Triad

The speaker also discusses the concept of the "Dark Triad," a term used to describe three personality traits that are characterized by manipulation, exploitation, and a lack of empathy. These traits are:

  • Narcissism: a grandiose sense of self-importance and a tendency to use others to achieve one's own ends.
  • Machiavellianism: a willingness to use manipulative and exploitative tactics to achieve one's goals.
  • Psychopathy: a lack of empathy, impulsivity, and a tendency to engage in antisocial behavior.

The Dangers of Emotional Intelligence

The speaker argues that emotional intelligence can be particularly problematic when combined with the Dark Triad traits. For example:

  • Emotionally intelligent psychopaths: can use their emotional intelligence to manipulate others while also lacking empathy and engaging in antisocial behavior.
  • Emotionally intelligent narcissists: can use their emotional intelligence to build a false sense of self-importance and exploit others to achieve their own ends.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the speaker argues that while emotional intelligence can be a valuable skill, it can also be misused by highly manipulative individuals. The Dark Triad traits of narcissism, Machiavellianism, and psychopathy can be particularly problematic when combined with emotional intelligence, and can lead to exploitation and manipulation of others.
